Based on section 4.6 of the README — **Customer Support & Service** — here are the user stories and acceptance criteria for each listed feature.

---

## Feature 1: Ticket management

**Story:** As an agent, I can create, assign, escalate, resolve, and close support tickets so that customer issues are tracked and resolved within a structured workflow.

Acceptance criteria:
- A ticket can be created manually by an agent from the ticket list view, from a contact detail view, or from the self-service portal by a customer. Required fields are subject, category, and contact. Priority defaults to medium if not set.
- Ticket status transitions follow a defined flow: Open → In Progress → Waiting on Customer → Resolved → Closed. A ticket can move back from Waiting on Customer to In Progress when the customer replies. A resolved ticket can be re-opened within 7 days if the customer indicates the issue is not fixed.
- Assigning a ticket to an agent sends that agent an in-app and email notification. The assigned agent can reassign to another agent or escalate to a manager from the ticket detail view.
- Escalation requires a mandatory escalation reason. On escalation the ticket priority is automatically raised by one level (e.g. medium becomes high) and the escalating agent's manager receives an in-app notification.
- Resolving a ticket requires a resolution note. The resolution note is visible to the customer in the self-service portal.
- Closing a ticket is distinct from resolving it. A resolved ticket auto-closes after 7 days if the customer does not re-open it. An agent can also close it manually at any time after resolution.
- A ticket can be merged into another ticket by a manager. On merge, all interactions, replies, and attachments from the source ticket are moved to the target ticket. The source ticket is soft-deleted with a reference to the target.
- A ticket can be split into two tickets by a manager. The original ticket retains all existing content. The new ticket is pre-populated with the subject and contact of the original and the agent selects which interactions to move across.
- Related tickets can be linked without merging. Linked tickets are visible in each other's detail view under a related tickets panel.
- All status changes, assignments, escalations, merges, splits, and closures are written to the audit trail.

## Feature 2: SLA engine

**Story:** As a support manager, I can configure SLA policies that automatically track response and resolution deadlines so that service commitments are met consistently and breaches are acted on immediately.

Acceptance criteria:
- SLA policies are configured per ticket category and optionally overridden per contact loyalty tier or account type, with loyalty tier taking precedence over account type.
- Each SLA policy defines a first response time and a resolution time expressed in business hours.
- Business hours are defined globally and can be overridden per SLA policy. Configuration includes active days of the week, start time, end time, and timezone.
- Public holidays can be configured as non-business days. On a configured holiday, SLA timers do not advance.
- When a ticket is created, the applicable SLA policy is determined and attached to the ticket. The calculated first response deadline and resolution deadline are stored on the ticket record and displayed as countdown timers on the ticket detail view.
- The first response timer is met when an agent posts the first outbound reply on the ticket. The resolution timer is met when the ticket moves to resolved status.
- SLA timers pause automatically when the ticket enters Waiting on Customer status and resume when the status returns to In Progress.
- A breach warning notification fires at 80% elapsed time for both the first response and resolution targets. The assigned agent and their direct manager receive an in-app and email notification.
- A breach is recorded when a deadline passes without the milestone being met. The breach flag is visible on the ticket card in list view and in the ticket detail view.
- Breached tickets are surfaced in a dedicated SLA breach list accessible to managers and admins, sortable by breach severity and time since breach.
- SLA metrics (breach rate, average first response time, average resolution time) are reported per category and per agent in the support analytics dashboard.

## Feature 3: Support categories and custom forms

**Story:** As a support manager, I can define support categories with custom intake forms so that tickets are created with the right information for each issue type from the start.

Acceptance criteria:
- Support categories are configured by admins in a hierarchical structure up to two levels deep (category and sub-category). Each category has a name, description, default priority, default assigned team, and an associated SLA policy.
- Each category can have a custom intake form. The form builder supports field types: text, textarea, number, date, dropdown, checkbox, and file upload.
- Required fields on a custom form must be completed before a ticket can be submitted. Incomplete forms show field-level validation errors.
- Custom forms are shown on the ticket creation screen when the agent selects a category. Switching category replaces the form. Previously entered values are cleared on category switch with a confirmation prompt.
- Custom form data is stored as structured JSON on the ticket record and rendered in a readable format in the ticket detail view under a form responses panel.
- The self-service portal displays the same category-specific form to customers when they create a ticket. Categories can be marked as agent-only and hidden from the portal.
- Category configuration changes apply to new tickets only. Existing tickets retain the form data captured at creation.
- Categories can be deactivated by admins. Deactivated categories do not appear in the ticket creation form but remain visible on historical tickets.

## Feature 4: Knowledge base

**Story:** As an agent, I can create and maintain knowledge base articles so that common issues are documented and customers and agents can find answers without raising tickets.

Acceptance criteria:
- Articles are created with a title, body (rich text supporting headings, lists, code blocks, images, and embedded links), category, and author. Status follows the content lifecycle: draft, in review, approved, published, archived.
- Only published articles are visible to customers in the self-service portal and to agents in the ticket search panel. Draft and in-review articles are visible only to agents and admins in the knowledge base management view.
- Articles are organised into categories. Categories support one level of nesting (parent and child). Each article belongs to exactly one category.
- The knowledge base is full-text searchable via Meilisearch through Laravel Scout. Search returns results ranked by relevance with the matching term highlighted in the excerpt.
- An agent can link a knowledge base article to a ticket. The linked article is visible in the ticket detail view and can be shared with the customer as part of a reply with a single click.
- Customers in the self-service portal can rate articles as helpful or not helpful. The helpful vote count and total view count are visible to agents and admins on the article detail view.
- An article with a helpful ratio below 40% (minimum 10 votes) is automatically flagged for review and its author notified.
- Articles are versioned. Each published update creates a new version. Admins can view version history and restore a previous version, which creates a new draft from the restored content.
- Article search is available within the ticket creation flow. Before submitting a ticket, the customer or agent is shown suggested articles matching the subject entered, reducing unnecessary ticket creation.

## Feature 5: Internal notes on tickets

**Story:** As an agent, I can add internal notes to a ticket that are visible only to other agents so that I can communicate context and reasoning without the customer seeing internal discussion.

Acceptance criteria:
- Any agent with access to the ticket can add an internal note. Internal notes are visually distinct from customer-facing replies in the ticket thread — they use a different background colour and carry an "internal" label.
- Internal notes are never visible to customers in the self-service portal regardless of ticket status.
- An internal note supports rich text formatting: bold, italic, lists, links, and file attachments up to 25MB stored in S3.
- An agent can @mention another agent in an internal note. The mentioned agent receives an in-app notification with a link directly to the note.
- Internal notes are included in the ticket export and in the audit trail.
- Internal notes can be edited by their author within 30 minutes of posting. After 30 minutes they are locked. Edits are indicated with an edited timestamp.
- Internal notes can be deleted by their author or a manager. Deleted notes are replaced with a deletion placeholder showing the actor and timestamp. The note body is not recoverable after deletion.
- The ticket detail view shows a toggle to hide or show internal notes so agents can view a clean customer-facing thread when needed.

## Feature 6: Customer satisfaction rating

**Story:** As a support manager, I can automatically send a satisfaction rating request to customers after ticket resolution so that I can measure support quality at the individual ticket level.

Acceptance criteria:
- A CSAT rating request is automatically sent to the contact via their preferred channel (email or in-app) when a ticket moves to resolved status. Sending is triggered as a queued job with a configurable delay after resolution (default 1 hour).
- The rating request presents a 1–5 scale. The customer selects a score. An optional free-text comment field is shown after score selection.
- Rating submission is a single-click action from email (each score is a separate tracked link) or an in-line widget in the portal. No login is required to submit a rating from email.
- A customer can only submit one rating per ticket. Re-clicking a rating link after submission shows a thank-you message.
- The submitted rating and comment are stored on the ticket record and visible in the ticket detail view.
- A rating of 1 or 2 automatically creates a follow-up activity assigned to the ticket's assigned agent with a due date of the next business day.
- CSAT ratings are excluded from tickets that were raised by the agent on the customer's behalf and marked as agent-created. This prevents skewing scores from non-customer-initiated tickets.
- Contacts who have opted out of support communications do not receive CSAT rating requests.
- Aggregate CSAT scores per agent, per category, and per time period are reported in the support analytics dashboard.

## Feature 7: Agent performance dashboard

**Story:** As a support manager, I can view an agent performance dashboard so that I can monitor team output, identify coaching needs, and report on support quality.

Acceptance criteria:
- The dashboard shows the following metrics per agent for the selected date range: tickets created, tickets resolved, tickets closed, average first response time, average resolution time, SLA breach count, breach rate percentage, and average CSAT score.
- Date range selector supports: today, yesterday, last 7 days, last 30 days, this month, last month, and custom range.
- Metrics are shown in a sortable table. Clicking an agent's row expands a detail panel showing their ticket list for the period, filterable by status and priority.
- A team-level summary row at the top of the table shows aggregate metrics across all agents in the selected team.
- A manager can filter the dashboard by team. Admins see all teams with a team selector dropdown.
- Trend indicators show whether each metric has improved or declined compared to the previous equivalent period (e.g. last 30 days vs the 30 days before that).
- The dashboard is not real-time — it refreshes on page load and can be manually refreshed. Data is cached for 15 minutes.
- The dashboard is exportable as a CSV or PDF report. The export includes all displayed metrics per agent for the selected date range.
- Access is restricted to manager role and above. Agents can view only their own metrics in a personal performance panel on their home dashboard.

## Feature 8: Canned responses library

**Story:** As an agent, I can select pre-written canned responses when replying to tickets so that common queries are answered quickly and consistently.

Acceptance criteria:
- Canned responses are created and managed by managers and admins. Each response has a title, body (rich text), category tag, and an active/inactive status.
- When composing a reply on a ticket, the agent can open a canned response picker by typing a slash command (/) or clicking a toolbar button. The picker searches by title and category tag as the agent types.
- Selecting a canned response inserts its body into the reply editor at the cursor position. The agent can edit the inserted text before sending.
- Canned response bodies support the same variable placeholders as email templates: contact first name, ticket number, agent name, and account name. Variables are resolved on insertion, not at send time.
- Agents can mark individual canned responses as personal favourites. Favourited responses appear at the top of the picker list.
- Usage count per canned response is tracked. Responses are sortable by usage count in the management view so popular responses are easily identifiable.
- Inactive canned responses do not appear in the picker. They remain visible in the management view and can be reactivated.
- Canned responses are searchable and filterable by category tag in the management view. An admin can bulk-deactivate responses by category.

## Feature 9: Email to ticket

**Story:** As a support manager, I can configure inbound email addresses that automatically create or update tickets so that customers can raise and respond to support issues by email without using the portal.

Acceptance criteria:
- One or more support email addresses are configured in system settings. Each address is mapped to a default ticket category and priority.
- An email sent to a configured support address creates a new ticket. The sender's email is matched to an existing contact. If matched, the ticket is linked to that contact. If not matched, a new contact record is created with the sender's email and name from the email headers, and the ticket is flagged for agent review.
- The email subject becomes the ticket subject. The email body becomes the first interaction on the ticket. Attachments are stored in S3 and linked to the ticket.
- A reply to the CRM's notification email for an existing ticket (identified by a ticket reference number in the subject line) appends the reply body as a new inbound interaction on the existing ticket rather than creating a new ticket.
- The ticket reference number is automatically included in the subject line of all outbound ticket notifications in the format [Ticket #XXXXX] so that reply threading works correctly.
- Inbound email processing is handled as a queued job. Processing delays exceeding 5 minutes trigger an admin alert.
- Spam and auto-reply detection: emails with auto-reply headers (Auto-Submitted, X-Autoreply) are discarded and not converted to tickets. A log entry is created for discarded emails visible to admins.
- An agent can reply to a ticket from within the CRM and the reply is sent from the configured support email address, maintaining the email thread from the customer's perspective.
