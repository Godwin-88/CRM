Based on section 4.9 of the specs — **Back-Office & Finance** — here are the user stories and acceptance criteria for each listed feature.

## Feature 1: Revenue tracking — invoices and payment status

**Story:** As a finance manager, I can create and track invoices linked to deals and accounts so that all revenue is recorded, monitored, and reportable from within the CRM.

Acceptance criteria:
- An invoice record can be created manually by an agent or generated automatically when a deal moves to Closed Won status, if the auto-generate invoice setting is enabled per pipeline in system settings.
- Each invoice requires: account, contact, line items (minimum one), currency, and due date. Invoice number is auto-generated following a configurable format (default: INV-{YYYY}-{sequential_number}, e.g. INV-2025-0001). The format is configurable by an admin.
- Line items each require a description, quantity, unit price, and tax rate. The system calculates line item total, tax amount, subtotal, total tax, and invoice total automatically. All calculations are performed server-side and returned to the frontend — no financial calculations are performed client-side.
- An invoice can be linked to one or more deals. Linking to a deal populates line items from the deal's associated products if any are set. The agent can add, edit, or remove line items after population.
- Invoice status lifecycle: Draft → Sent → Partially Paid → Paid → Overdue → Cancelled. The Draft → Sent transition is triggered manually by the agent. The Sent → Overdue transition is triggered automatically by the nightly scheduler when the due date passes without full payment recorded.
- Payment records are attached to invoices. Each payment requires: amount, payment date, payment method (bank transfer, card, mobile money, cash, or other), and an optional reference number. Multiple partial payments are supported. The invoice status advances to Partially Paid when the sum of payments is greater than zero but less than the invoice total, and to Paid when the sum equals or exceeds the invoice total.
- Mobile money is a supported payment method given the Kenya market context. The reference number field is used to record the M-Pesa transaction code.
- A PDF invoice is generated on demand using DomPDF and stored on the R2 disk at the path `invoices/{account_id}/{invoice_id}/INV-{number}.pdf`. The PDF is served via 15-minute signed URLs using the same R2 pattern as contract PDFs.
- An invoice can be sent to the contact via email directly from the CRM. The email includes the invoice PDF as an attachment fetched from R2 and a payment summary. Sending is logged as an outbound interaction on the contact record.
- Overdue invoices trigger an automated overdue reminder to the contact via their preferred channel. Reminders fire at 1 day, 7 days, and 14 days after the due date. Reminder intervals are configurable per account type in system settings.
- Invoice records are soft-deletable. Soft-deleted invoices are excluded from all finance reporting but retain their R2 PDF files.
- All invoice create, update, status change, and payment record actions are written to the audit trail via Spatie Activitylog.
- Invoice creation and editing require the `invoices.manage` permission. Payment recording requires `invoices.payments`. Both are assigned to finance manager and admin roles by default.

## Feature 2: Ledger summary per account

**Story:** As an account manager, I can view a financial ledger summary for any account so that I have an immediate picture of that account's revenue history, outstanding balance, and payment behaviour.

Acceptance criteria:
- The account detail view includes a financial summary panel showing: total invoiced (all time), total paid, total outstanding (invoiced minus paid), count of overdue invoices, and average payment delay in days (average of actual payment date minus due date for paid invoices).
- A full ledger view is accessible from the financial summary panel. It shows all invoices for the account in a chronological list with invoice number, date, due date, total, amount paid, outstanding balance per invoice, and status badge.
- The ledger list is filterable by invoice status, date range, and currency.
- A payment history tab within the ledger view shows all payment records across all invoices for the account, ordered by payment date descending, showing invoice number, payment amount, payment method, payment date, and reference number.
- Currency handling: if an account has invoices in multiple currencies, the ledger shows each currency separately. No automatic currency conversion is performed. A currency filter is available to view one currency at a time.
- The ledger summary panel is visible to agents assigned to the account, their managers, and admins. Finance managers see all accounts' ledgers regardless of assignment.
- Ledger data reflects posted invoices and payments only. Draft invoices are excluded from all ledger totals.
- The ledger view is exportable as CSV. The export includes all ledger rows matching the current filter state.

## Feature 3: Procurement — purchase orders and vendor assignment

**Story:** As a finance manager, I can create purchase orders and assign them to vendors so that procurement spend is tracked and authorised within the CRM.

Acceptance criteria:
- A purchase order (PO) record requires: PO number (auto-generated, format PO-{YYYY}-{sequential_number}), vendor (from the vendor registry), line items (description, quantity, unit price, tax rate), currency, and required by date.
- PO status lifecycle: Draft → Submitted → Approved → Partially Received → Received → Cancelled. The Draft → Submitted transition is triggered by the requesting agent. Submitted → Approved requires approval from a user with the `procurement.approve` permission.
- Approval workflow: on submission, the approver (configurable per PO category in system settings) receives an in-app and email notification. The approver can approve or reject from the notification or from the PO detail view. Rejection requires a mandatory rejection reason and returns the PO to Draft status.
- A PO value threshold is configurable by an admin (default KES 100,000). POs below the threshold are auto-approved on submission. POs at or above the threshold require manual approval.
- Goods receipt is recorded against a PO. Each receipt record specifies which line items were received and in what quantity. A PO advances to Partially Received when some but not all line items are fully received, and to Received when all line items are fully received.
- A PO can be linked to one or more vendor invoices received. Linking a vendor invoice to a PO enables three-way matching: PO quantity vs received quantity vs vendor invoice quantity. Mismatches are flagged visually on the PO detail view.
- PO PDF is generated on demand using DomPDF and stored on R2 at `procurement/{vendor_id}/{po_id}/PO-{number}.pdf`. Access via 15-minute signed URLs.
- All PO actions (create, submit, approve, reject, receive, cancel) are written to the audit trail.
- PO creation requires the `procurement.create` permission. Approval requires `procurement.approve`. Both are restricted to finance manager and admin roles by default.

## Feature 4: Vendor management

**Story:** As a finance manager, I can maintain a vendor registry with performance ratings and manage vendor requests so that supplier relationships are organised and evaluated consistently.

Acceptance criteria:
- A vendor record requires: name, category (goods, services, or both), primary contact name, primary contact email, and primary contact phone. Optional fields are registration number, tax identification number, bank details (stored encrypted using Laravel's `encrypted` cast), physical address, and website.
- Bank details fields (account name, account number, bank name, branch code, SWIFT code) are stored using the `encrypted` cast. They are displayed only to users with the `vendors.financials` permission. All other users see these fields masked.
- Vendor status: active, inactive, or blacklisted. Blacklisted vendors cannot be assigned to new purchase orders. Attempting to assign a blacklisted vendor returns a validation error.
- A vendor can be rated after each completed PO. Ratings are on a 1–5 scale across four dimensions: quality, delivery timeliness, communication, and value for money. An overall rating is the average of the four dimensions.
- Rating history is maintained per vendor showing all ratings with the rating date, PO reference, and rater. The vendor detail view shows the overall average rating and a breakdown per dimension as a star display.
- Vendor requests are a lightweight RFQ (request for quotation) flow. A vendor request is created with a description of goods or services needed and sent to one or more vendors. Each vendor responds via a unique email link or through a portal stub. Responses are recorded on the request record.
- A vendor can have attached documents (certificates, contracts, compliance documents). Documents are stored on R2 at `vendors/{vendor_id}/documents/{filename}` and accessed via 15-minute signed URLs. Document uploads use Spatie Media Library configured to the `r2` disk.
- Vendor records are soft-deletable. Soft-deleted vendors are excluded from PO assignment but their history is retained.
- All vendor record changes are written to the audit trail.
- Vendor management requires the `vendors.manage` permission, assigned to finance manager and admin roles by default. Viewing vendor records (excluding financials) is available to all authenticated users.

## Feature 5: Asset inventory

**Story:** As an operations manager, I can maintain an asset register with stock levels and asset assignments so that the organisation's physical and digital assets are tracked and accounted for.

Acceptance criteria:
- An asset record requires: asset name, asset type (hardware, software, vehicle, furniture, or custom), serial number or identifier, purchase date, purchase cost, currency, and current status (available, assigned, under maintenance, disposed).
- Asset types are configurable by an admin. Custom asset types can be added without code changes.
- An asset can be assigned to a user (internal assignment) or to an account (client-facing asset). Assignment requires a start date and an optional expected return date. An asset in assigned status cannot be assigned to a second assignee without first being returned.
- Return of an asset is recorded with a return date and a condition note. On return the asset status reverts to available unless the condition note triggers a maintenance flag, in which case status moves to under maintenance.
- Stock-level tracking is supported for bulk consumable assets (e.g. SIM cards, USB drives, printed materials). A stock asset record has a total quantity, available quantity, and minimum threshold. When available quantity falls below the minimum threshold, the asset owner receives an in-app notification.
- Asset assignment history is maintained per asset showing all assignees, assignment dates, return dates, and condition notes in chronological order.
- Depreciation is calculated for assets with a configurable useful life in years using straight-line depreciation. Current book value is displayed on the asset detail view as: purchase cost minus (annual depreciation × years since purchase).
- Asset documents (purchase receipts, warranty certificates, insurance documents) are stored on R2 at `assets/{asset_id}/documents/{filename}` and accessed via 15-minute signed URLs via Spatie Media Library on the `r2` disk.
- An asset register export is available to operations managers and admins as a CSV containing all asset records with their current status, assigned user or account, book value, and last assignment date.
- All asset record changes, assignments, and returns are written to the audit trail.
- Asset management requires the `assets.manage` permission, assigned to operations manager and admin roles. Viewing the asset register requires `assets.view`, assigned to all authenticated users.

## Feature 6: Basic HR module

**Story:** As an HR manager, I can maintain an employee directory and track headcount so that organisational structure is visible within the CRM and agent records are linked to their HR profiles.

Acceptance criteria:
- An employee record is created automatically when a new CRM user account is created. The employee record is linked one-to-one with the user record via a `user_id` foreign key.
- Additional HR fields on the employee record are: employee number (auto-generated), department, job title, employment type (full-time, part-time, contract, intern), start date, end date (nullable), reporting manager (self-referencing FK to users), and employment status (active, on leave, terminated).
- The employee directory is a searchable list of all active employee records showing name, employee number, department, job title, and reporting manager. Search covers name, employee number, and job title via Meilisearch through Laravel Scout.
- An org chart view renders the reporting hierarchy as a collapsible tree. The tree is built from the reporting manager relationships on employee records. Maximum depth is 8 levels. Trees deeper than 8 levels are truncated with a load more option.
- Headcount planning is supported via department headcount records. Each department has a configured headcount target. The directory shows current headcount vs target per department in a summary panel.
- When a CRM user account is deactivated, the linked employee record's employment status is automatically set to terminated and the end date is set to the deactivation date.
- Employee documents (contracts, offer letters, ID copies) are stored on R2 at `hr/{employee_id}/documents/{filename}` and accessed via 15-minute signed URLs. Access to HR documents requires the `hr.documents` permission, restricted to HR manager and admin roles.
- Personal employee data (salary, bank details, national ID) is outside the scope of this module. The HR module covers organisational structure and directory only.
- Access to the employee directory requires the `hr.view` permission, assigned to all authenticated users by default. Editing employee records requires `hr.manage`, assigned to HR manager and admin roles.
- All employee record changes are written to the audit trail.

## Feature 7: Banking relationship notes

**Story:** As a finance manager, I can record notes and context about the organisation's banking relationships so that key banking contacts, terms, and correspondence are accessible within the CRM.

Acceptance criteria:
- A banking relationship record requires: institution name, relationship type (current account, credit facility, overdraft, trade finance, or treasury), relationship manager name, relationship manager email, and relationship manager phone.
- Additional fields are: account number (stored encrypted using the `encrypted` cast), credit limit (for credit facility and overdraft types), facility expiry date, and interest rate.
- Encrypted fields (account number) are visible only to users with the `banking.financials` permission, assigned to finance manager and admin roles. All other users see the field masked.
- A banking relationship has a notes thread identical in behaviour to the legal matter notes thread — append-only, rich text, timestamped, and immutable after posting.
- Documents relating to the banking relationship (facility letters, term sheets, bank statements) are stored on R2 at `banking/{relationship_id}/documents/{filename}` and accessed via 15-minute signed URLs via Spatie Media Library on the `r2` disk. Document access requires the `banking.documents` permission.
- A facility expiry date within 60 days triggers an automated in-app and email notification to the finance manager and admin.
- Banking relationship records are not linked to contacts or accounts — they are organisation-level records accessible from the back-office module only.
- All banking relationship record changes, note additions, and document uploads are written to the audit trail.
- Access requires the `banking.view` permission, assigned to finance manager and admin roles by default. Creating and editing requires `banking.manage`, restricted to admin role.

## Feature 8: Financial analytics dashboard

**Story:** As a finance manager, I can view a consolidated financial analytics dashboard so that revenue, outstanding balances, and procurement spend are visible in one place.

Acceptance criteria:
- The financial analytics dashboard shows the following metric tiles for the selected date range: total invoiced, total collected (sum of payments received), total outstanding (invoiced minus collected), collection rate percentage (collected divided by invoiced), overdue invoice count and value, and total PO spend (sum of approved and received PO values).
- A revenue trend chart shows monthly invoiced vs collected amounts as a dual-line chart for the trailing 12 months.
- An accounts receivable aging panel shows outstanding invoice values bucketed as: current (0–30 days overdue), 31–60 days, 61–90 days, and over 90 days. Each bucket shows total value and invoice count. Clicking a bucket opens a filtered invoice list for that aging band.
- A top 10 accounts by outstanding balance table shows account name, total invoiced, total paid, outstanding balance, and oldest unpaid invoice date. The table is sortable by outstanding balance descending by default.
- A procurement spend panel shows total PO spend by vendor category for the selected period as a bar chart, and a top 5 vendors by PO value table.
- All dashboard metrics are filterable by date range, currency, and account type.
- Dashboard data is calculated by the nightly analytics job and cached. The last calculation timestamp is displayed on the dashboard. A manual refresh button busts the cache and recalculates immediately as a queued job, notifying the finance manager when recalculation is complete.
- The financial analytics dashboard requires the `analytics.finance` permission, assigned to finance manager and admin roles by default.
- All dashboard metrics are exportable as a single CSV. A formatted PDF report is also available via the report builder.

## Feature 9: Accounting system integration hooks

**Story:** As a finance manager, I can synchronise invoice and payment data with an external accounting system so that the CRM and the accounting system remain consistent without manual double-entry.

Acceptance criteria:
- Integration connectors are available for Xero, Sage, and QuickBooks. Each connector is configured in the integrations settings panel with OAuth2 credentials and a sync direction setting (CRM to accounting, accounting to CRM, or bidirectional).
- The OAuth2 authorisation flow for each accounting provider is completed within the CRM. The access token and refresh token are stored encrypted in the `integration_configs` table using the `encrypted` cast.
- Sync events are triggered in two ways: automatically on specific CRM events (invoice created, invoice status changed, payment recorded) via a queued job on the `integrations` Horizon queue, and manually via a sync now button in the integration settings panel.
- For CRM to accounting sync: invoices in Sent, Partially Paid, Paid, and Overdue status are pushed to the accounting system. Each invoice is mapped to the accounting system's invoice object. Line items, tax rates, and payment records are included in the mapping.
- For accounting to CRM sync: payment records created in the accounting system against a synced invoice are pulled back to the CRM and recorded as payment records on the matching invoice. Invoice status is updated accordingly.
- A sync log is maintained per integration showing each sync event with: timestamp, direction, record type, record ID, success or failure status, and the raw response from the accounting provider (stored as JSON, visible to admins only).
- Sync failures are retried three times with exponential backoff on the `integrations` queue. Persistent failures after three retries trigger an in-app and email notification to the admin with the error detail from the accounting provider response.
- The integration uses the `HttpClientService` circuit breaker. If the accounting provider returns 5 consecutive failures within a 10-minute window, the circuit opens and no further sync attempts are made for 30 minutes. The admin is notified when the circuit opens and when it closes.
- Contact and account data is not synced to accounting systems — only invoices and payments. Account mapping between CRM accounts and accounting system customers is maintained in a configurable mapping table in the integration settings panel.
- Disconnecting an integration from the settings panel revokes the OAuth token with the provider, deletes the stored tokens from `integration_configs`, and stops all scheduled sync jobs for that integration. Historical sync logs are retained.
- Integration configuration and the sync log require the `integrations.manage` permission, assigned to admin role only.
