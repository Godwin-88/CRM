Based on section 4.5 of the specs — **Customer Experience & Loyalty** — here are the user stories and acceptance criteria for each listed feature.

---

## Feature 1: Loyalty program configuration

**Story:** As an admin, I can configure loyalty programs with tiers, points rules, and redemption rules so that the business can reward and retain customers systematically.

Acceptance criteria:
- An admin can create one or more named loyalty programs. Each program has a name, description, currency label (e.g. "points", "stars", "miles"), and an active/inactive status.
- A program defines a set of tiers. Each tier requires a name, a minimum points threshold, and an optional set of benefits (free-text descriptions). Default tiers are Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum but are fully customisable.
- Points earning rules are configurable per program. Supported rule types are: points per currency unit spent (linked to a closed deal or invoice value), points per interaction type completed, points for profile completion, and bonus points on specific dates or events.
- Each rule has a multiplier field allowing double or triple points to be configured for promotional periods without creating a new rule.
- Redemption rules define how points are spent. Supported redemption types are: discount voucher generation, free product assignment, and tier upgrade credit. Each redemption type requires a minimum points threshold.
- A program can have an expiry policy: points never expire, expire after N months of inactivity, or expire on a fixed calendar date per year.
- Only one loyalty program can be active per contact at a time. If multiple programs exist, the contact is enrolled in the program matching their account type or segment, configurable in program settings.
- Program configuration changes (new rules, modified thresholds) apply to future transactions only. Historical points balances are not recalculated.

## Feature 2: Points ledger and tier management

**Story:** As an agent, I can view a contact's points balance, transaction history, and current tier so that I can answer loyalty queries accurately and take action on their behalf.

Acceptance criteria:
- Each contact enrolled in a loyalty program has a points ledger showing every credit and debit transaction with the date, description, points amount, running balance, and the event that triggered it.
- The ledger is paginated at 25 rows per page and sortable by date.
- The contact's current tier, points balance, and points needed to reach the next tier are displayed prominently on the contact detail view loyalty panel.
- Tier upgrades are evaluated automatically after each points-earning transaction. If the new balance meets or exceeds the next tier's threshold, the contact is upgraded immediately and a tier upgrade notification is triggered.
- Tier downgrades are evaluated by the nightly scheduler. If a contact's balance has fallen below their current tier's threshold (due to expiry or redemption), they are downgraded to the appropriate tier.
- An agent can manually credit or debit points on a contact's ledger with a required reason note. Manual adjustments are logged in the audit trail with the agent's ID, timestamp, and reason.
- Manual adjustments require the `loyalty.adjust` permission, assigned to manager role and above by default.
- A contact can be enrolled in or unenrolled from a loyalty program by a manager. Unenrolment retains the ledger history but freezes the balance and prevents further earning or redemption.

## Feature 3: Customer incentive communications

**Story:** As a marketing manager, I can configure automated communications that notify contacts of their loyalty status, rewards, and incentives so that customers stay engaged with the program.

Acceptance criteria:
- Automated incentive communications are triggered by loyalty events: tier upgrade, tier downgrade, points earned (configurable threshold, e.g. every 100 points), points expiry warning (configurable days before expiry), and redemption confirmation.
- Each trigger has a configurable communication template per channel (email, SMS, in-app). Templates support loyalty-specific variables: current tier, points balance, points earned in transaction, next tier name, points to next tier, and expiry date.
- Communications are sent via the Notification service and dispatched as queued jobs. Delivery status is tracked and visible on the contact's interaction timeline as an automated incentive interaction type.
- A contact can opt out of loyalty communications independently of their general marketing opt-out. The loyalty opt-out field is visible and editable on the contact's communication preferences panel.
- Contacts opted out of loyalty communications still earn and redeem points — only the automated messaging is suppressed.
- Incentive communication templates are subject to the content lifecycle management workflow — they must be approved before activation.
- A manager can trigger a one-off incentive communication to a specific contact or segment outside of the automated triggers, using the campaign send flow with an incentive-type template.
- Delivery failures for incentive communications are retried three times. Persistent failures are surfaced in the notification error log accessible to admins.

## Feature 4: NPS and CSAT survey engine

**Story:** As a customer experience manager, I can create and send NPS and CSAT surveys to contacts so that I can measure satisfaction and act on feedback systematically.

Acceptance criteria:
- The survey engine supports two survey types: NPS (Net Promoter Score, 0–10 scale) and CSAT (Customer Satisfaction, 1–5 scale). Both types support an optional open-text follow-up question.
- Surveys are created with a name, type, question text, optional follow-up question, and a target segment or individual contact list.
- Surveys are sent via email or SMS. Email surveys embed the rating scale directly in the email body as clickable links. SMS surveys send a tracked link to a mobile-optimised survey landing page hosted by the CRM.
- A contact's survey response is recorded against their contact record and linked to the survey. The response includes the numeric score, the open-text answer if provided, the response timestamp, and the channel through which it was submitted.
- NPS responses are automatically categorised as Promoter (9–10), Passive (7–8), or Detractor (0–6). The NPS score for the survey is calculated as percentage Promoters minus percentage Detractors.
- CSAT score is calculated as the percentage of responses scoring 4 or 5 out of total responses.
- A contact who has already responded to a survey cannot submit a second response. Re-visiting the survey link shows a thank-you message.
- Survey results are displayed in an analytics view showing score breakdown, response rate, open-text responses in a scrollable list, and trend comparison to the previous survey of the same type.
- Surveys respect the contact's communication opt-out preferences. Opted-out contacts are excluded from survey sends automatically.

## Feature 5: Customer expectation management and SLA definitions

**Story:** As a customer experience manager, I can define service level expectations per customer tier or account type so that agents and customers have a consistent, agreed standard of service.

Acceptance criteria:
- SLA definitions are configured per support category and optionally overridden per loyalty tier or account type. An SLA definition specifies first response time and resolution time in business hours.
- Business hours are configurable globally and overridable per SLA definition. Supported settings are: specific days of the week, start and end time, and timezone.
- When a ticket is created, the system assigns the applicable SLA based on the contact's loyalty tier and ticket category, in that priority order. The assigned SLA is visible on the ticket detail view.
- SLA targets are displayed as countdown timers on the ticket. The first response timer starts when the ticket is created. The resolution timer starts when the ticket moves to in-progress.
- An SLA breach warning fires when 80% of the allowed time has elapsed without the milestone being met. The assigned agent and their manager receive an in-app and email notification.
- An SLA breach is recorded when the milestone is not met by the deadline. The breach is flagged on the ticket and recorded in the SLA analytics report.
- SLA timers pause when a ticket is in a waiting-on-customer status. The timer resumes when the ticket moves back to in-progress.
- SLA definitions can be published to a customer-facing page (configurable URL) so that customers can view the service commitments applicable to their tier.

## Feature 6: Customer onboarding workflows

**Story:** As an agent, I can initiate and track a structured onboarding workflow for a new customer so that every new contact goes through a consistent, documented onboarding experience.

Acceptance criteria:
- Onboarding workflow templates are configured by admins. Each template contains an ordered list of steps. Each step has a name, description, assigned role (e.g. account manager, technical support), and optional due date offset from enrolment (e.g. step 3 is due 7 days after enrolment).
- A workflow is initiated from a contact or account detail view. The agent selects the appropriate template and confirms enrolment. Enrolment creates an onboarding record linked to the contact or account.
- On enrolment, the system creates an activity for each step assigned to the relevant agent role, with due dates calculated from the enrolment date.
- The onboarding progress view shows all steps, their assigned agents, due dates, and completion status in a checklist format. Overall percentage completion is displayed as a progress indicator.
- An agent marks a step as complete from the onboarding view or from the activity. Completion requires a completion note.
- Overdue steps trigger an automated reminder notification to the assigned agent and a summary to the account manager daily until completed.
- A welcome email sequence is automatically triggered on onboarding enrolment, using the configured welcome sequence drip from the campaign module.
- Completing all steps automatically advances the contact's type from prospect to customer if it is not already set.
- Onboarding analytics show average completion time per template, step-level drop-off rates, and overdue step frequency across all active onboardings.

## Feature 7: Self-service portal

**Story:** As a customer, I can log into a self-service portal to view my interaction history, tickets, documents, and loyalty balance so that I can find information and take basic actions without contacting an agent.

Acceptance criteria:
- The self-service portal is a separate authenticated web interface distinct from the agent CRM. Contacts log in using their email address and a one-time password sent to their registered email or phone.
- On login, the portal displays the contact's open and resolved tickets, recent interactions, active contracts and documents available for download, and their loyalty balance and tier.
- A contact can submit a new support ticket from the portal. The ticket creation form shows categories relevant to their account type. Submitted tickets appear in the agent CRM inbox immediately.
- A contact can reply to an existing open ticket from the portal. The reply is appended to the ticket as an inbound interaction and notifies the assigned agent.
- Documents available for download are contracts and any files explicitly shared with the contact by an agent. Agents mark files as customer-visible from the CRM.
- The portal displays the contact's communication preferences and allows them to update their marketing opt-in, loyalty communications opt-in, and preferred channel.
- Session timeout for the portal is 30 minutes of inactivity. On timeout the contact is redirected to the login page.
- All portal actions (login, ticket submission, document download, preference update) are logged in the audit trail against the contact record with a portal source indicator.
- The portal is mobile-responsive and meets WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards.

## Feature 8: Guided self-service journeys

**Story:** As a customer experience manager, I can configure guided step-by-step journeys on the self-service portal so that customers can complete common tasks independently without agent assistance.

Acceptance criteria:
- Guided journeys are configured by admins in the CRM. A journey has a name, a trigger (customer selects it from the portal menu), and an ordered list of steps.
- Each step contains a title, instructional content (rich text), and an optional action type: information only, form input, document download, or external link.
- Form input steps capture structured data from the customer (e.g. update billing details, submit a meter reading). Submitted data is saved to the contact's custom fields or creates a pre-populated ticket depending on the step configuration.
- The customer progresses through steps sequentially. They cannot skip to a later step. They can go back to a previous step and change their input before final submission.
- On journey completion, a summary of the customer's inputs is displayed and a completion interaction record is created on their contact record in the CRM.
- An agent receives an in-app notification when a customer completes a journey that has the notify agent on completion flag enabled.
- Journeys can be published and unpublished by admins. Unpublished journeys do not appear in the portal menu.
- Journey completion rate (completions divided by starts) is tracked per journey and visible in the experience analytics dashboard.

## Feature 9: Contact reactivation campaigns

**Story:** As a marketing manager, I can automatically identify and target dormant contacts with reactivation campaigns so that lapsed customers are re-engaged without manual list building.

Acceptance criteria:
- A reactivation trigger is configurable per contact type. The trigger fires when a contact has had no recorded interaction, activity, or deal movement for a configurable number of days (default 90 days).
- When the trigger fires, the contact is automatically enrolled in the configured reactivation drip sequence, if one is active and the contact is not already enrolled in any other active sequence.
- The reactivation sequence is a standard drip sequence (built in Feature 6 of section 4.4) tagged as reactivation type. It supports all standard sequence actions.
- A contact who responds to any step of the reactivation sequence (opens email, clicks link, replies to SMS, raises a ticket) is automatically exited from the sequence and flagged as re-engaged. Their assigned agent receives an in-app notification.
- Re-engaged contacts have their inactivity timer reset. They will not be re-enrolled in the reactivation sequence again until the configured inactivity period elapses again from the re-engagement date.
- A reactivation analytics view shows: total contacts currently in reactivation sequences, re-engagement rate (re-engaged divided by enrolled), and revenue recovered from re-engaged contacts within 60 days of reactivation.
- Contacts who complete the full reactivation sequence without responding are tagged with a configurable dormant label and removed from active outreach lists unless manually re-added.
- Reactivation trigger configuration and sequence assignment is restricted to manager role and above.

## Feature 10: CLV analytics module

**Story:** As a sales manager, I can view customer lifetime value analytics for my contact base so that I can prioritise high-value relationships and identify at-risk accounts.

Acceptance criteria:
- CLV is calculated per contact as the sum of all closed won deal values attributed to that contact over their lifetime with the business.
- Predicted LTV is calculated using a configurable model. The default model is average deal value multiplied by estimated deal frequency multiplied by estimated customer lifespan in years. All three inputs are derivable from historical data per contact type and are overridable per contact.
- The CLV analytics view shows: top 20 contacts by CLV, average CLV by contact type, average CLV by loyalty tier, CLV distribution histogram, and a cohort retention table showing how CLV evolves over the customer's first 12 months.
- A churn risk score is displayed per contact. The score is derived from days since last interaction, open ticket count, NPS score, and deal activity recency. Score thresholds for low, medium, and high churn risk are configurable by admins.
- Contacts in the high churn risk band are surfaced in a dedicated at-risk contacts list on the CLV dashboard, accessible to agents and managers.
- CLV and churn risk scores are recalculated nightly by the scheduled analytics job. The last calculation timestamp is shown on the dashboard.
- The CLV dashboard can be filtered by contact type, loyalty tier, account, owner, and date range.
- CLV data is exportable as CSV from the dashboard. The export includes contact ID, name, historical CLV, predicted LTV, current tier, churn risk score, and last interaction date.
- CLV analytics are visible to agents (own contacts only), managers (team contacts), and admins (all contacts).
